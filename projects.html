Compound Machine Project

Class: Honors Principles of Engineering

Date: September 7th, 2020 - October 11th, 2021

The objective of this project was to create a compound machine that uses at least four different simple machines, to accomplish a task. First, my group and I decided on the four different simple machines we would use (inclined plane, pulley, axle driving wheel, and first class lever). We then decided that the task to complete would be raising up keys. This would help older people who are not flexible enough to bend down have easier access to their keys. Throughout the project, I primarily focused on building. I helped build the axle driving the wheel, the first-class lever, and the inclined plane. Two of my other partners also helped greatly in the building process, with my third partner focusing primarily on the project documentation. The challenges we faced during this project were having the right side of the first-class lever stay lifted up when a weight was dropped on the left side, and placing all the simple machines in the right place so everything would work as designed. To overcome the challenge with the first class lever, we taped a small counterweight to the left side of the first-class lever. This allowed the left side to stay down and the right side to stay up when a bigger weight was dropped on the left side. To overcome the challenge with the placement of the simple machines, we used trial and error. We adjusted all the simple machines and tested, until the task was completed consistently. As the project progressed, we all had great communication, planned effectively, and also utilized the time we had in class. When one of us was stuck on something, the others always helped. This project taught me how to combine different simple machines to complete a task, effectively plan out a project or task, and draw detailed/accurate sketches.

hangman

Class: AP Computer Science Principles

Date: January 15th, 2020 - January 29th, 2020

The objective of this project was to create a hangman game that met all the requirements said in the AP Create Task Rubric. Me and my partners plan was to have the user guess a letter. If they get a letter correct the word with that letter filled in will appear in the terminal. Then they are asked if they want to guess the whole word which they can answer yes or no to. If they answer no they are asked to guess another letter, but if yes they enter their guess of the whole word. If the word is correct then the game ends, if it isn't the amount of incorrect guesses they have left is reduced by one. If the letter guessed is incorrect then the user is told their guess was wrong, and the amount of incorrect guesses they have left is reduced by one. If the amount of incorrect guesses reaches is 0 or if they guess all the letters in the word, the game ends. In this project I did almost all the programming, while my partner did most of the documentation. After we both finished our parts we both gave tips to each other on how to improve, which we used to improve our code and documentation. Throughout the project we only had one challenge which was meeting the requirements said in the AP Create Task Rubric, but we overcame this by meeting for an hour outside of class and brainstorming ideas on how to meet the requirements. Throughout the project me and my partner stayed in contact with each other, threw texts, listened to each other's ideas, and helped each other out when one of us was stuck. When one of us was too busy the other took leadership and did the tasks that needed to be done. I believe this teamwork and leadership was the reason we were able to create a high quality project in a short amount of time. Working on this project taught me how to document better and the importance of open-mindedness.

firefighters

Class: AP Computer Science Principles

Date: October 9th, 2020 - November 15, 2020

The objective of this project was to create a project to meet all the requirements said in the AP Create Task Rubric. Me and my partner decided to create a firefighter animation where a helicopter is dumping water on a fire extinguishing it with the help of a firefighter. I programmed the trees, helicopter, the helicopter dumping water on fire animation, and the ending screen. While my partner programmed the fire, firefighters with hose, fire trucks, and finally the background. We faced many challenges throughout the course of the project. Big ones were finding times that work for both of us since we both were very busy and making the animations and drawings look as realistic as possible. To overcome the time challenge we both texted each other constantly to try and make times to meet up and to show what each of us had programmed or done on our own. To overcome the challenge of the drawings not looking realistic enough, we redid some of the drawings and animations. Throughout this project we had great communication, great conversations, and we both listened to each other's ideas. When one of us was stuck on a program the other took leadership and helped out the other. We were both able to depend on each other. This project taught me so many things such as the importance of teamwork, importance of communications, and to utilize the resources around you such as teachers and peers.
